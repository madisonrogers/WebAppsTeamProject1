Volleyball

Mines Volleyball is coached by Jamie Skadeland who finished her 10th season as the head volleyball coach – and 11th year overall – at Colorado School of Mines in 2017-18. Skadeland spent the 2007 season as an assistant coach for the Orediggers before taking over as the head coach in 2008.

Through 10 seasons at the helm, Skadeland has guided Mines to a 232-81 overall record (.741 winning percentage), as well as a 148-36 mark in RMAC matches (.804 winning percentage). Under her direction, Mines won four consecutive RMAC regular season championships (2012-15), the program’s first ever RMAC Tournament Championships (2015 and 2016) and advanced to nine straight NCAA tournaments.

Skadeland brought Mines its fifth RMAC regular season championship in 2017 as the Orediggers recorded at least 25 wins for a sixth-straight season, going 25-6 overall and 17-1 in RMAC play. The Orediggers were ranked as high as No. 16 in the AVCA Top 25 and finished the season ranked No. 21 in NCAA Division II. Mines qualified for their ninth-straight NCAA tournament as the fourth seed in the South Central Region and advanced to the second round of the tournament thanks to a first-round win over fifth-seeded Angelo State.

Press-Release 12/05/2017

Colorado School of Mines senior setter Marina Hansen has been named an all-American by both the AVCA and D2CCA. Hansen earned a first-team nod from the AVCA, and is a second-team selection by the D2CCA. Hansen was also named First-Team All-Region by both organizations. Mines has now had five student-athletes earn 11 AVCA All-American selections in program history, all coming since 2011. She is the third AVCA First-Team selection for the Orediggers, joining Melanie Wannamaker (2013) and Danielle Johnson-Hazlewood (2014). Hansen led the RMAC and the region in assists per set at 12.96, a clip that puts her second in NCAA Division II. A five-time RMAC Setter of the Week this season and the RMAC Setter of the Year, Hansen was also one of the RMAC's top servers at 0.35 aces per set. Her setting helped power the Mines offense to national top-10 statistical rankings in hitting percentage (.272, 8th), kills per set (14.91, 5th), and assists per set (14.07, 4th)

Softball

Mines was chosen second in the RMAC preseason coaches' poll, announced on Monday. The Orediggers (32-15 in 2017) earned 103 points in the poll to sit second behind Colorado Mesa, which earned 121 points and 11 first-place votes. MSU Denver (102), Regis (90), and Chadron State (80) round out the top five.

Mines is coming off a terrific 2017 season that saw them finish second in the RMAC regular-season standings while advancing to the RMAC Tournament title game. The Orediggers went 32-15 overall under RMAC Coach of the Year Leah Glasgow, their best record since 2013, and advanced to the RMAC Tournament final for the first time since that same year.

Mines is looking to pick up where they left off and compete for an RMAC title. This weekend (2/24-2/25), Mines plays Fort Lewis for their first weekend of RMAC competition.

Basketball

Mines basketball is coached by Pryor Orser, who has been the head coach for basketball for 16 years, alongside his assistant coaches Brad Schick and Scott Hill. Since Orser started coaching in 2001, his coaching record is 292-173, and has earned 4 RMAC Coach of the Year honors.

Mines basketball has an impressive list of records and achievements: having 7 all-American athletes, a winning streak record of 17 games, winning season crowns for 2010-11, 2011-12, 2014-15 and 2016-17, and more. You can read more about their full accomplishments and team/player statistics here.
